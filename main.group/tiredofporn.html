Are you tired of porn?

You're not alone. 40 million Americans regularly visit porn websites. If you search the Web for the term "tired of porn" you'll get tens of millions of results.

Growing weary of pornography makes sense — for a lot of reasons ...

After all, people are created for relationship. That's why we play team sports. That's why we have friends. That's why we get married. We are designed to experience fullness of life and intimacy through our relationships. Porn works against that natural inclination and need. Pornography is usually a habit experienced alone, often in secret, detached from the depth and connection of relationships.

Though it is often a solitary experience, the effects of porn reach far beyond a single person. One recent study indicates that 56% of divorces cite porn as a factor in the failure of the marriage.

So, maybe it's not just that you're tired of porn. Maybe you're actually tired of pornography's effect on your life. Maybe you're tired of the secrets, or the hiding, or the effect it's having on your relationships.

Maybe there's something better

Sometimes when you're tired of something, it's because you're ready to be done with it. Sometimes you're tired because you sense that there's something better — something more fulfilling — waiting just around the corner. Maybe it's time to turn the corner …
